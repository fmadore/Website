# Impeccable

This file holds the design context for the Impeccable skill. Every design skill (`/impeccable craft`, `/impeccable layout`, `/impeccable typeset`, etc.) reads this section before doing any work.

## Design Context

### Users

Primary visitor is an **academic peer** — a fellow researcher in Islamic studies, African studies, or digital humanities. They arrive with specific intent: find a publication, verify a citation, skim the CV, check on a current project, or evaluate collaboration potential. Secondary audiences (press, funders, students) matter but do not drive design decisions.

Use context:

- Desk-based, larger screens, short sessions between other work.
- More scanning than reading — typographic hierarchy has to do the navigating.
- They trust rigor over flash; a templated aesthetic actively undermines credibility with this audience.

The job-to-be-done: _"I need to find and cite Madore's work, or see what he's currently working on, quickly and without friction, and I want the site to corroborate that he's a serious person."_

### Brand Personality

**Ink + Signal: the press archive, read computationally.** Three-word distillation: _documentary, exacting, alive._

The site's identity comes from the collision of its two source materials: the West African press corpus the scholar studies (nameplates, section rules, typeset bibliographies) and the computational layer he builds (structured metadata, corpus counts, machine-readable everything). Neither half is decoration for the other — the page itself demonstrates the method: primary sources set with editorial gravity, apparatus set in a machine voice.

Emotional targets:

- Visitors feel they are inside a working archive, not on a personal-brand page.
- The masthead asserts; the apparatus reassures. Confidence from scale and rules, credibility from precision.
- Everything is ink on paper or light on microfilm — nothing is "web chrome."

### Aesthetic Direction

**Commit to the collision; retire tasteful restraint.** The previous direction (warm paper + ink-blue + editorial serifs) was a genre — recognisably "thoughtful academic site" — rather than an identity. Ink + Signal keeps the paper ground and the editorial discipline but makes the two halves of the work visible and loud where it counts.

**Mood references (direction):**

- Newspaper nameplates and post-independence West African press typesetting — heavy rules, capital mastheads, dated columns.
- Critical editions and typeset bibliographies — the apparatus as an aesthetic in itself.
- Microfilm readers — the dark theme is a negative, not a "dark mode."
- Contemporary editorial-brutalist web (sharp corners, visible structure), tempered by book-quality serif text.

**Anti-references (things to avoid):**

- Templated academic CMS sites and faculty-directory defaults.
- AI-slop tells: gradients of any kind, glassmorphism, glow, `border-left` accent stripes, rounded cards with soft shadows.
- Code-editor/terminal aesthetics — the mono voice is _metadata_, never body copy, never a whole-page theme.
- Soft-launch minimalism: acres of whitespace, single-column feature pages, hero photos with overlay text.

**The two voices.** Every element on every page belongs to exactly one voice:

1. **The document voice** — Archivo (display) and Newsreader (prose). Titles, abstracts, descriptions, quotes, captions. Serif italic for subtitles and asides.
2. **The data voice** — Spline Sans Mono, small, letterspaced, often uppercase. Eyebrows, datelines, counts, filters, ledger labels, tags, DOIs, pagination, footer legal. If a string could live in a database column, it is set in the data voice.

Never blur the voices: no mono headlines, no serif metadata.

**Typography path.** Settled stack: **Archivo** (variable; nameplate at `wdth` ~123 / weight 830–850, section heads at `wdth` ~112 / weight 750–800), **Newsreader** (all prose, opsz axis on; italics carry subtitles and captions), **Spline Sans Mono** (data voice only). All three ship full Latin Extended for French and West African diacritics. Retired: Fraunces, Spectral, Commissioner, JetBrains Mono — with them goes the "knowing exception" clause; Archivo/Newsreader were chosen against the reflex lists on their own merits (a grotesque cut for headlines; a serif designed for news text).

**Color path.** Two inks on two grounds, one accent:

- **Daylight (light):** warm paper `#FAF7EF`, ink `#191509`, muted ink `#5C5442`, faint ink `#93896F`, hairline `#DCD4BE`, raised paper `#FFFDF7`, borders `#C9C0AA`.
- **Midnight (dark) — the microfilm negative:** warm near-black `#171310`, cream `#EFE7D6`, muted cream `#B3A88D` / `#CBC0A6` (long prose `#DDD2B8`), faint `#7E7460`, hairline `#383126`, borders `#453D2F`/`#5A5140`.
- **Accent: pine (warm teal)** — `#1E6A56` on daylight, brightened to `#4FBB99` on midnight. It marks _the current thing_: active nav, eyebrows, №-markers, key numbers, primary actions, the newest year-bar. One accent; the earlier ink-blue, amber, and press-vermillion are all retired.

Apply by weight: ground ≫ ink ≫ accent. The accent never fills large areas except the single primary action button. New colors, if ever needed (data-viz), are derived in OKLCH from these anchors.

**Structure & rules.** Hierarchy is drawn, not floated:

- **5px / 4px heavy rules** open mastheads; **3px rules** open major sections; **1px hairlines** separate entries. Rules are ink-colored (cream in midnight), never gray.
- **№-numbered sections** (`№ 01` in accent mono + Archivo heading) are the standard content module on every page.
- **The ledger** is the universal record idiom: hanging mono key/date column left, serif content right, hairline between rows. Publications, CV entries, metadata panels, activities, and facets are all ledgers.
- **Flat chips** (1px border, sharp corners, mono caps, count appended) for tags, filters, and type counts. Selected state = solid ink fill.
- **Corners are square. Shadows do not exist. Glass does not exist.** Depth comes from ink density and rule weight.

**Data as ornament.** The only decoration permitted is real data made visible: year-distribution bar strips (ink bars, accent on the current year), corpus counts, frequency-scaled key-term lists. If a flourish doesn't encode something true, it goes.

**Imagery.** Photographs, covers, scans are plates: square corners, 1px border, mono or serif-italic caption below (`Fig. 1 — …`). Scans from the corpus are first-class imagery; prefer them to stock anything.

**Motion.** Nearly none. Instant state changes for filters and theme; at most a short fade on page enter. The register is print, not app.

**Theme strategy.** Daylight and midnight are both first-class and both _warm_: midnight is the same page photographed as a negative — grounds swap warm-black, type swaps cream, the pine accent brightens. It is not slate, not blue, not an inversion filter. Every component is specified in both palettes above; no component ships with only one.

### Design Principles

1. **Two voices, strictly cast.** Document voice (Archivo/Newsreader) for what the scholar writes; data voice (Spline Sans Mono) for what the machine indexes. Every string belongs to one. Blurring them is the system's only unforgivable error.

2. **Hierarchy is drawn in rules.** Before adjusting size or color, reach for the rule system: 5px masthead, 3px section, 1px hairline. The page should still be navigable if all type were the same size.

3. **The ledger is the default record.** Anything that is a dated or keyed record — publication, appointment, activity, metadata field — renders as a hanging-column ledger row, not a card.

4. **Pine marks the present.** The accent means "current, active, newest, primary." If it appears more than a handful of times per screen, it means nothing — demote occurrences until it's scarce.

5. **Data is the only ornament.** Year-bars, counts, and term frequencies may decorate because they are true. Nothing else may.

6. **Print register, both themes.** Square corners, no shadows, no glass, no glow, near-zero motion — in daylight _and_ midnight. Midnight is a microfilm negative of the same printed page, designed as its own pass.

7. **Peer-respecting density.** Scholars scan; don't under-fill pages. Dense, well-typeset information signals seriousness and respects the reader's time. Resist the urge to pad with whitespace when structured information would serve better.
