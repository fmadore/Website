# Impeccable

This file holds the design context for the Impeccable skill. Every design skill (`/impeccable craft`, `/impeccable layout`, `/impeccable typeset`, etc.) reads this section before doing any work.

## Design Context

### Users

Primary visitor is an **academic peer** — a fellow researcher in Islamic studies, African studies, or digital humanities. They arrive with specific intent: find a publication, verify a citation, skim the CV, check on a current project, or evaluate collaboration potential. Secondary audiences (press, funders, students) matter but do not drive design decisions.

Use context:

- Desk-based, larger screens, short sessions between other work.
- More scanning than reading — typographic hierarchy has to do the navigating.
- They trust rigor over flash; a templated aesthetic actively undermines credibility with this audience.

The job-to-be-done: _"I need to find and cite Madore's work, or see what he's currently working on, quickly and without friction, and I want the site to corroborate that he's a serious person."_

### Brand Personality

**Warm, humanist, grounded.** Three-word distillation: _considered, humane, rigorous._

The site should feel made by a careful person, not generated by a template. Closer in feel to an essayist-scholar's journal than an institutional bio page: editorial-magazine seriousness combined with letterpress warmth. Rigor without coldness. Asymmetric layouts welcome. This is a working historian's site — a Francophone Canadian studying Islam in West Africa while based in Germany — not a university's faculty directory entry.

Emotional targets:

- Visitors feel they're in the hands of a person, not a CMS template.
- The site whispers "serious scholarship" while staying warm to read.
- Depth and texture over shine and gradient.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Evolve the current system, don't reset it.** The current glassmorphism-heavy, teal-and-amber direction reads as templated; the goal is to quiet the glass, warm the palette, and choose typography with more character — while preserving enough continuity that returning visitors aren't disoriented.

**Mood references (direction):**

- Editorial-magazine web presences (LRB / n+1 restraint, not a magazine site-builder template).
- Letterpress and fine-book typography — serifs with ink and warmth.
- Independent scholar-essayist sites (the Craig Mod / Robin Sloan lineage, adapted for an academic portfolio).
- Subtle paper/ink tactility — suggestive, not skeuomorphic.

**Anti-references (things to avoid):**

- Templated academic CMS sites (Divi/Squarespace academic themes, Cambridge faculty-directory defaults).
- AI-slop tells: blue-to-purple gradients, cyan-on-dark, glowing dark-mode accents, gradient text, side-stripe `border-left` accents, glassmorphism everywhere.
- Generic "digital humanities" styling — monospace body copy as shorthand for "technical," code-editor dark themes, terminal aesthetics.
- Institutional chrome — deep hero banners, centered three-column grids, "Our Approach / Our Work / Our Team" patterns.

**Theme strategy.** Light and dark are both first-class — every component must be designed twice, not bolted-on-dark. Light should feel like good paper: warm off-whites, ink-black text, neutrals tinted toward warm brown rather than cool gray. Dark should feel like a reading lamp after dusk: warm dark surfaces, not corporate dark-blue. Neither theme is the afterthought.

**Typography path.** Current `IBM Plex Sans` is on Impeccable's reflex-rejection list and should be replaced with something more humanist and character-rich. `Source Serif 4` is serviceable but not distinctive — reconsider alongside. Pair one distinctive display/serif face with a refined workhorse sans for UI/body. Monospace is reserved for code blocks and data-viz chrome (map legends, chart labels) — never body copy.

**Color path.** Move away from the templated teal+amber. Warm the neutrals (tint toward ink and warm brown, not cool gray). Keep a single confident accent rather than teal-primary + amber-highlight + multiple scheme colors running in parallel. Apply the 60-30-10 rule by visual weight: neutral paper / secondary ink / rare accent. OKLCH for all new color work; reduce chroma at extreme lightness.

**Motion.** One well-orchestrated page-enter with staggered reveals. Scroll-reveals stay but quieter. No bounce / elastic easing — real objects decelerate smoothly. Motion should feel like pages turning, not like a web app responding.

**Layout.** Asymmetry and editorial composition over center-aligned three-column grids. Vary spacing for rhythm — don't apply the same padding everywhere. Cap body text at ~65–75ch. Break the grid intentionally for emphasis (pull-quotes, margin notes, single large figures).

### Design Principles

Apply these to every design decision made under Impeccable:

1. **Typography does the heavy lifting.** Before reaching for color, shadow, or ornament, see what hierarchy-through-type can solve. This is a writer's site — type is the primary material.

2. **Warm neutrals, rare accents.** The dominant surface should feel like paper, not cool gray. The accent earns its place by being scarce — one confident color, used with intent, not three in parallel.

3. **Quiet the glass.** Glassmorphism remains only where it genuinely reinforces depth or context (sticky header over imagery, modal overlays). Remove it from cards, callouts, and decoration.

4. **Asymmetry over center alignment.** Academic sites default to a centered hero plus three-column grid. This one should feel written, not laid out — left-aligned, varied rhythm, unexpected but purposeful compositions.

5. **Both themes, equal craft.** Every component is designed twice. Dark is not "light with inverted colors" — it is its own pass. Warm dark, not corporate dark.

6. **No AI tells.** Reject Impeccable's absolute bans (gradient text, `border-left` accent stripes) and the reflex font list (Inter, Plex, Fraunces, Playfair, DM Sans, etc.). If the first instinct matches a default, look further.

7. **Peer-respecting density.** Scholars scan; don't under-fill pages. Dense, well-typeset information signals seriousness and respects the reader's time. Resist the urge to pad with whitespace when structured information would serve better.
